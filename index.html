About

A brief look into my story

  • 2018-2019

    Initial Interest

    I started out my professional career as a technician working on motorcycles, off-road vehicles, and other repair jobs, and I enjoyed what I did. I also realized that the particular line of work I was in wasn't lucrative enough for my far reaching goals, and I began to explore other options. After some hit or miss ideas, I had a few friends introduce me to coding, and I was pretty leery at first, as the whole thing seemed incredibly alien. After a couple tutorials, and looking at all the learning opportunities, I fell in love. I started researching bootcamps and universities, found General Assembly, and the rest is history.

  • December 2019

    A Developer is Born

    In December of 2019 I was accepted into General Assembly's Remote Software Engineering Immersive course. I began feeling very comfortable, and short of a couple very challenging days, remained extremely comfortable throughout the course in areas where others struggled. I finished all assignments with 100 percent completion, and focused on areas that I wasnt as familiar with, even if the course didn't specifically call for it.

  • March 2020

    A New Fontier

    In March of 2020 I completed Generally Assembly's software engineering course and look forward to all future posibilities. Continuing to update both past projects, and my current knowledge in the industry, I search for the opportunity to join an organization that willl be a good fit for me, and the potential team. I'm excited to continue to build on my existing skills, and contribute to a future employer's needs to the utmost of my abilities. If I've sparked your interest, feel free to send me an e-mail, or connect with me on Linkedin. I would love the opportunity to discuss potential job offers, or even the state of the industry and your feelings on the matter. Thank you for your time!

Total Garage

View past repairs on a specific model and year of car, listed in an easy to view range of mileages.

This app was created using pair programming, in order to view a range of repairs around a particular mileage. Used by users potentially looking for common issues, or a prospective car buyer. App uses full authentication in order to create, uptdate, or delete an entry. Backend authorization uses JWT tokens with a refresh token good for 24 hours after login. Seperately deployed backend contains user info as well as all repair entries.
